One of the most famous is the Casentinesi Forest National Park, located in the northern part of the country, between Tuscany and Emilia-Romagna regions. The Casentinesi Forest National Park is a vast protected area, covering over 36,000 hectares of pristine wilderness. The park is known for its rugged mountain landscapes, dense forests, and crystal-clear streams and rivers. It is home to a rich variety of wildlife, including wolves, deer, wild boars, and several species of birds of prey.
